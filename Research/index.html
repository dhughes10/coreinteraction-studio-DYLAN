O-R-G

O-R-G is a software company that started specifically on the first business day of the year 2000. The company designs and programs websites, apps, screensavers, and smaller collections of code. O-R-G start off originally collaborating with other design studios such as Project Projects and MGMT Design. The company eventually followed its own path with its single employee and founder, David Reinfurt, who intended the company to appear as a large corporate firm. This assumption has given Reinfurt his company the credibility of a large firm for good business.

David Reinfurt is a graphic designer and writer who is based in New York City. Before O-R-G he studied at North Carolina University and Yale and then eventually began working at a design and consulting firm in San Fransisco as an interaction designer. There he was a lead designer for the MTA vending machines. Moving forward Reinfert starts O-R-G.

In 2006, Reinfurt joins with Stuart Bailey who is also a graphic designer to which they establish Dexter Sinister. Dexter Sinister is a workshop located on the Lower East Side where Reinsert and Bailey developed a model of efficiency for large scale publishing. Dexter Sinister is know for publishing its magazine DotDotDot until 2011. Reinfurt and Bailey then established the Serving Library with Angie Keefer who is a writer. The Serving Library is an archive of resourceful publications that provides library like space.
